Lines of Action

Lines of Action (LOA) is a board game invented by Claude Soucie.

Rules
  • Two players take turns, each moving a piece to capture opposing piece.
  • The goal of the game is to form a contiguous region with all of one's pieces
  • The game ends when one of the two players forms a contiguous region with all pieces
  • A player is allowed to move vertically, horizontally, or diagonally
  • They are allowed only to move with a magnitude equal to the number of pieces in the straight line along the board
  • A player is allowed to jump over friendly pieces but not enemy pieces
  • A player is allowed to end on an enemy square to "capture" it, but can't end on a friendly piece
  • If the final move of a player forms a contiguous region for both the player and their opponent, player who made the move wins

    • Controlling Play
  • Press the HELP button to gain information about play and features
  • Black Player has the ability to toggle between manual and automatic play for black player
  • White Player has the ability to toggle between manual and automatic play for white player
  • Undo button undoes the last two moves on the board
  • New game starts a new game on the board
  • Quit will close out of the current game

    • Additional Information
  • The automatic player utilizes the heuristic and alpha-beta pruning found in the Board class
